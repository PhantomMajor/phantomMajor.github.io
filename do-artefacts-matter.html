Do artefacts matter?

May 2026

Most knowledge workers in my surroundings use Generative AI to create artefacts. Pitch decks, market research, product requirements document, test cases, and even end software is partially or fully generated using AI.

The cost of generating artefacts, in the knowledge-economy, has collapsed. Consequently, the entire ecosystem has become noisy, with average to low quality content. 2025's word of the year, "slop", comes close to the word "spam". Both are high-volume low-value content; have diluted usefulness. Additionally, slop recursively feeds to models, making the quality regress further1.

If artefacts have become cheap and abundant, does it even make sense to make them? Artefacts must pass either of two criteria to matter. They should either be useful, or they must satisfy the human desire to create as an end in itself.

In the midst of slop, usefulness needs to be evaluated with even more care and skepticism. What matters even more now is not the raw output but the judgement. One way to cultivate this judgement is by deliberate practice of evaluation of artefacts.

Another fundamental reason is the human drive to create something good - as an end in itself, as an art. Here as well, constant practice of creation of artefacts improves the art. This has been validated across cultures, geographies, and times. AI reduces the cost of creation, but does not harvest the skill of judgement and discrimination on its own.

Deliberate and constant practice requires seriousness. It starts with a consideration of the real purpose of the artefact. Next is exercising the process of creation till something is created. This artefact is then evaluated against reality. These observations & reflections form new knowledge, assumptions, and a stronger drive towards building the next version till something good has been created.
