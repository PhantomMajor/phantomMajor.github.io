The thing about writing is that it forces you to think

Most people treat writing as a way to record thoughts they've already had. But real writing — the kind that teaches you something — works the other way. You start with a vague idea and the act of putting it into sentences forces the idea into a sharper form.

This is why keeping a blog is valuable even if nobody reads it. The discipline of writing regularly changes how you think. You notice things you wouldn't have noticed. You form opinions you wouldn't have formed. Some of the best essays were never meant to be published.

The hard part isn't writing — it's deciding what's worth saying. Once you have something you genuinely believe, the words follow more easily than you'd expect.
